## Ownership

You own your inputs and outputs. OpenAI assigns all rights in outputs to you.

## Training on Your Data

**API data is NOT used for training by default.** This is a key difference from ChatGPT consumer usage.

- API inputs and outputs are not used to train models unless you explicitly opt in.
- ChatGPT (consumer) data may be used for training unless you opt out.
- Zero data retention (ZDR) is available for API usage.

## Data Retention

- **API default:** 30 days (for abuse monitoring), then deleted.
- **Zero data retention:** available — no storage at all.
- Specific endpoints may have different retention (check developer docs).

## What You Can Do

- Store session data locally — you own it.
- Use outputs commercially.
- Share and publish outputs (subject to the Sharing & Publication Policy, which requires disclosure that content is AI-generated in some contexts).

## What You Cannot Do

- Build competing AI products using the API or outputs.
- Use outputs to train competing models.
- Violate the Usage Policies (similar to other providers: no illegal activity, weapons, CSAM, etc.).

## Notable Points

1. **API vs ChatGPT distinction is critical.** API data is not trained on by default; ChatGPT consumer data may be.
2. **Regional privacy policies differ.** US and rest-of-world have separate privacy policies.
