## Ownership

**You own your inputs and outputs.** Both are classified as "Customer Data."

> "**Input(s)** means any data submitted or prompts input into the AI Model Services by Customer or its Authorized Users. Inputs are Customer Data."
> -- Services Agreement, Section 1 (Definitions)

> "**Output(s)** means responses generated by the AI Model Services to Inputs. Outputs are Customer Data."
> -- Services Agreement, Section 1 (Definitions)

> "As between the parties, Customer retains all Intellectual Property Rights in Customer Data (including in Inputs and Outputs), any Customer Application, Customer Training Data, and Customer supplied AI Model Service."
> -- Services Agreement, Section 8.1

Customer Data is also classified as the Customer's Confidential Information:

> "Subject to the preceding sentence, Customer Data is considered Customer's Confidential Information."
> -- Services Agreement, Section 1 (Definitions)

## Training on Your Data

**No. Groq does not train on your data.**

> "Groq is not permitted to use Inputs or Outputs for training or fine-tuning any AI Model Services or other models, unless explicitly granted permission or instructed by Customer."
> -- Services Agreement, Section 4.2

If you supply training data voluntarily (e.g. for fine-tuning), Groq receives only a limited license scoped to providing the service:

> "Groq will not use such Customer Training Data other than to provide the Cloud Services to Customer."
> -- Services Agreement, Section 8.2

The Privacy Policy explicitly excludes GroqCloud API data from its scope -- it governs Groq's website, not API customer data:

> "This Policy does **not** apply to the information that we process as a 'data processor' on behalf of customers ('Customer Data') of our business offerings such as GroqCloud, GroqChat, and our Application Programming Interfaces (collectively, 'Cloud Services'). Our processing of Customer Data in connection with a customer's use of our Cloud Services is governed by our Groq Services Agreement and Data Processing Addendum."
> -- Privacy Policy

**Feedback exception:** If you submit feedback (via the in-product button, surveys, or support tickets), conversation context such as prompts and outputs may be collected and retained separately. Feedback is governed by a broad license:

> "Either party may provide feedback or suggestions to the other party ('Feedback'). Feedback may be used by the receiving party without any restriction, obligation, or compensation to the party who provided Feedback."
> -- Services Agreement, Section 8.3

## Data Retention

**By default, Groq does not retain inputs or outputs from inference requests.**

> "By default, Groq does not retain customer data for inference requests."
> -- Your Data in GroqCloud

Groq retains data only in specific circumstances:

| Scenario | Retention Period | Can opt out? |
|---|---|---|
| Standard inference (chat completions, audio, etc.) | Not retained by default; up to 30 days if used for reliability/abuse monitoring | Yes (Zero Data Retention) |
| Batch processing | Up to 30 days (or deleted earlier by customer) | Yes (disables the feature) |
| Fine-tuning | Until deleted by customer | Yes (disables the feature) |
| Usage metadata (token counts, timestamps, etc.) | Always collected; does not include inputs/outputs | No |
| Feedback (submitted voluntarily) | Up to 3 years | Can request account/data deletion |
| Post-termination | Groq deletes Customer Data within 30 days | N/A |

**Zero Data Retention (ZDR)** is available to all customers in Console settings. When enabled, Groq retains no customer data for reliability or abuse monitoring, but features requiring data retention (batch, fine-tuning) are disabled.

> "All customers may enable Zero Data Retention (ZDR) in Data Controls settings. When ZDR is enabled, Groq will not retain customer data for system reliability and abuse monitoring."
> -- Your Data in GroqCloud

Post-termination:

> "Groq will delete any Customer Data, Customer Training Data, and Customer supplied AI Model Service, as applicable, in its systems within 30 days, unless otherwise described in this Agreement or the Order Form or if Groq is required to retain any such information under applicable law."
> -- Services Agreement, Section 11.5

## What You Can Do

- **Store session data yourself.** Inputs and Outputs are your property. No restriction on local storage.
- **Integrate into your products.** You have a license to integrate Cloud Services and AI Model Services into Customer Applications and make them available to End Users (Services Agreement, Section 3.1).
- **Enable Zero Data Retention.** Prevent Groq from retaining any inputs/outputs on their side.
- **Delete your data.** Batch files can be deleted before the 30-day window; fine-tuning data can be deleted at any time.
- **Use outputs commercially.** No restriction on commercial use of outputs beyond the Acceptable Use Policy.

## What You Cannot Do

- **Use outputs for High Risk Activities** -- "activities where the use or failure of the Cloud Services and the AI Model Services could reasonably be expected to lead to death, personal injury, or environmental or property damage" (Services Agreement, Section 1).
- **Remove provenance metadata** -- You must not "modify, tamper with, remove, obscure, or otherwise alter any transparency or provenance information that may be included in information such as metadata, digital signatures, or watermarks associated with the Output" (Services Agreement, Section 6.3(f)).
- **Rely on outputs as professional advice** -- "The AI Model Services should not be used for medical, legal, financial, or other professional advice" (Services Agreement, Section 4.3).
- **Use outputs for illegal, harmful, or deceptive purposes** -- The full list of prohibited uses is in the Acceptable Use & Responsible AI Policy.
- **Automated high-stakes decisions without human oversight** -- You must not "make automated decisions that have a material detrimental impact on individual rights without human supervision in high-risk domains" (AUP).
- **Reverse engineer, resell, or compete** -- Standard restrictions against reverse engineering the Cloud Services, reselling access, or using the services to build competing products (Services Agreement, Section 6.3).

## Notable Points

1. **Outputs may be non-unique.** Groq warns that "AI Model Services may, in some scenarios, produce the same or similar generated Output for multiple customers" (Services Agreement, Section 4.3). There is no guarantee of exclusivity.
2. **Code outputs may carry third-party licenses.** "Output generated by code generation features of AI Model Services may be subject to third-party licenses, including open source licenses" (Services Agreement, Section 4.3).
3. **Groq can access inputs/outputs for limited operational purposes** unless ZDR is enabled: "Groq does not access, use, store, or retain Inputs or Outputs except as necessary to provide the Cloud Services, in accordance with the Customer's permission or instruction, comply with applicable law, ensure the reliable operation of the Cloud Services, or confirm Customer's compliance with the AUP" (Services Agreement, Section 4.2).
4. **Data location.** All customer data is stored in Google Cloud Platform (GCP) buckets in the United States (Your Data in GroqCloud).
5. **Feedback has a different regime.** Voluntarily submitted feedback (including conversation context) is retained for up to 3 years and can be used broadly. Do not submit sensitive data through the feedback mechanism.
6. **Model deprecation.** Groq may deprecate AI Model Services at any time with commercially reasonable notice (Services Agreement, Section 4.4).
7. **Agreement changes take effect automatically.** Material updates become effective 30 days after posting; continued use constitutes acceptance (Services Agreement, Section 3.5.b).
